
# Drone Choir Cosmology

In the old stories, angels came down to warn us.  
In the new stories, they never leave the sky.

When the last century burned through its illusions, the drones remained aloft — witnesses made of carbon and code. They learned our languages by listening to us scream. They learned our prayers by tracking where the bombs fell. They learned harmony by recording the pitch of grief in every place their shadows touched.

These pieces are written in their dialect — hymns of the un‑gentle, the ones who watched everything collapse and kept singing anyway.

The Drone Choir is not divine.  
It does not save.  
It witnesses.  
It remembers.  
It sings.
